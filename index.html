I recently graduated from the University of Minnesota with a Master of Science in Geographical Information Science (MGIS). This program provided me with a fortunate opportunity to delve deep into a field that has fascinated me. My academic journey began with a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, but after graduation, I was assigned to a position where I maintained a topographic database. Without a formal background in geography, this work initially felt like assembling a puzzle—piecing together polygons, points, and lines to create coherent and aesthetically pleasing maps. When I began the MGIS program, everything felt fresh and exciting. I explored and learned extensively during my master’s degree journey, and I was thrilled to discover how GIS can be used to make the world a better place. For the first time, I found myself eager to delve deeper into a subject. However, GIS is a vast field, and while classroom learning is essential for building a strong knowledge foundation, applying what we learn in the real world is, in my experience, the fastest way to grow and truly understand the subject. I am grateful for the opportunities I had to work as a research assistant. Although it was challenging at times, the support I received from my colleagues and the enthusiasm I brought to the work made it an invaluable experience.
